— Artist Statement

I am a fashion designer and wearable artist completing my final major studio evaluations at BNU Lahore. My practice exists at the intersection of haute couture, structural draping, and non traditional material experimentation. I view garments not as decorative apparel but as anatomical sculptures designed to communicate architectural form, emotional depth, and cultural identity.

— Work Style

My work is deeply physical, tactile, and intuitive anchored on direct form manipulation, structural pinning, and artisan engineering. Whether transforming industrial denim into fluid curves or crafting metallic wearable sculptures, my process values craftsmanship over automated reproduction. I work across fashion design, wearable art, photography, and conceptual installation.

— Aesthetics

Stark, moody, and intentionally impactful. My work operates on high-contrast emotional variables pairing dark, dramatic concepts with fluid textiles, experimental print, and aggressive metallic accents. I am drawn to themes of transformation, identity, vulnerability, and the tension between softness and structure.

Food Design

Veer Zaara — Gastronomic Storytelling

This project is a conceptual gastronomic storytelling experience inspired by the Bollywood film Veer Zaara, where cinema is reimagined through food, emotion, and visual design. The project explores how a menu can become a narrative medium by transforming iconic moments, relationships, and emotional themes from the film into a carefully curated five-course dining experience. Each course was inspired by a different stage of Veer and Zaara's journey — from their first meeting and the cultural connection between India and Pakistan, to themes of separation, sacrifice, patience, longing, and eventual reunion.

The dishes were designed with symbolic ingredients, textures, and presentations that reflected the emotional depth of the story. Elements such as jaggery and salt represented bittersweet love and divided borders, while slow-cooked dishes and layered desserts symbolized years of waiting, memory, and unspoken emotions. Alongside the food design, the visual identity of the menu was developed using aged paper textures, vintage typography, and nostalgic imagery to capture the timeless essence of the film.
Conceptual

Tiffin — Memory & Invisible Labor

This project explores the lunchbox as a vessel of memory, identity, care, and invisible labor. Rooted in the everyday culture of tiffins within homes, schools, and workplaces, the project investigates how food carries emotional histories, routines, sacrifices, and unspoken acts of love. What may appear to be an ordinary packed meal often holds stories of motherhood, responsibility, affection, financial circumstances, cultural values, and personal memory. The project aims to make visible the emotional and physical labor hidden behind these everyday acts of caregiving, especially the work performed by women within domestic spaces that often goes unnoticed and unacknowledged.

As part of the research process, I created a questionnaire and interviewed different women about the lunchboxes they prepared for their children and families. The final execution combines food, storytelling, textiles, illustration, and visual documentation to transform ordinary lunchboxes into intimate narrative archives.
Wearable Art

Body Extension — Wearable Sculpture

This project explores the idea of body extension as both a physical transformation and a personal response to insecurity and self-perception. The wearable finger extensions were hand-crafted as sculptural pieces designed to elongate the appearance of the hands, inspired by my own insecurity about having hands that felt too small and short. By extending the fingers through exaggerated metallic nail forms, the project transforms the body into something more powerful, dramatic, and visually amplified, turning a personal vulnerability into a conceptual design statement.

The sharp, elongated structures challenge conventional ideas of beauty and proportion while also functioning as an emotional armor. The extensions alter the movement and presence of the hands, making them appear more dominant, elegant, and expressive. Through this project, I explored how wearable objects can reshape the relationship between the body and identity, using design as a way to confront insecurities and reinterpret them through experimentation and form.
Draping Study

Experimental Draping Study

This project is an experimental draping study exploring sculptural form, volume, and silhouette through intuitive fabric manipulation. The garment was created entirely by hand directly on the mannequin without stitching or conventional pattern-making, using only draping, folding, gathering, and pinning techniques. By allowing the fabric to guide the process organically, the piece developed into a fluid yet structured form that emphasizes movement, texture, and spatial experimentation.

The exaggerated folds and asymmetrical volumes transform the garment into a three-dimensional sculptural surface, distorting the natural silhouette and creating a balance between softness and structure. Through this process, the project explores draping not only as a technical skill but also as an expressive and artistic design method, where fabric becomes a medium for experimentation, transformation, and form-building.
Photography

Old Lahore — Street Food Photography

This photography project documents the atmosphere, labor, and sensory experience of Old Lahore's food streets through candid visual storytelling. Focusing on local food vendors, traditional cooking methods, and the environments surrounding them, the project captures the energy and authenticity embedded within Lahore's street food culture. Through close-up compositions, steam-filled frames, textures, and moments of preparation, the photographs highlight the relationship between food, people, and everyday urban life.

The series explores the behind-the-scenes process, the intensity of large-scale cooking, the worn utensils, and the human presence behind every meal. From simmering pots and freshly cooked parathas to quiet details within the food stalls, the images aim to preserve the atmosphere and visual richness of Old Lahore's culinary spaces. The warm tones, documentary-style framing, and focus on ordinary moments create a sense of nostalgia and realism.
Textile Design

Period Poverty — Fashion as Social Commentary

This project explores the issue of period poverty through the intersection of printmaking, textile design, and draped fashion. The concept was developed around the silence, stigma, and lack of accessibility surrounding menstruation, particularly for women who experience financial, social, and cultural barriers related to menstrual health. I created an original print inspired by the emotional and physical realities associated with menstruation, combining abstract organic forms, layered textures, splashes, stains, and delicate floral imagery to symbolize the contrast between pain and softness, discomfort and femininity, visibility and suppression.

After developing the print, it was digitally transferred onto fabric and transformed into a sculptural draped garment created entirely on the mannequin without stitching. The garment was formed through draping, wrapping, pleating, and fabric manipulation, allowing the textile print itself to become the primary storytelling element. The project also explores how fashion and textiles can be used as mediums for social commentary and awareness rather than purely aesthetic objects.
Couture

Serpent Queen — Narrative Fashion

This project is a narrative-based fashion exploration inspired by the mythology and symbolism of snakes, particularly the idea of a serpent queen who represents power, transformation, seduction, and rebirth. The garment was developed as a character-driven visual story, combining fashion, printmaking, and textile experimentation to create a dark yet regal aesthetic. Inspired by serpentine textures, fantasy narratives, and mythical femininity, the project explores the snake not only as a symbol of danger, but also as a representation of resilience, protection, and feminine strength.

As part of the process, I designed and developed an original print inspired by snake skin textures, scales, shadows, and organic flowing forms. Deep reds, blacks, and dark maroon tones were used to evoke intensity, mystery, royalty, and danger. The outfit consists of a structured corset-inspired top paired with a flowing skirt and a sheer veil-like outer layer. Military-inspired elements were also incorporated, adding a sense of armor and authority to the overall character.
Photography

Pottery Workers — Documentary Photography

This photography project documents the lives and labour of local pottery workers, focusing on the physical process of shaping clay as well as the environment in which these artisans work. Through close-up details, textures, and environmental shots, the series captures the relationship between human hands, earth, movement, and tradition. The photographs explore pottery not only as a craft, but as a form of storytelling and cultural heritage that has been passed down through generations.

The project highlights the contrast between the beauty of handmade craftsmanship and the reality of the labour behind it. The series includes intimate photographs of hands moulding clay on the wheel, textures of wet earth, spinning pottery forms, and the surfaces of unfinished ceramic pieces. Through this series, I wanted to bring attention to the emotional and economic realities of artisans whose work is deeply connected to culture and tradition, yet often remains underappreciated.
Final Year Thesis — BNU Lahore

HYPNOTIC ENCHANTMENT

The collection explores how our perception of the world evolves with time revealing that the stories and symbols we encounter in childhood often hold deeper and more complex meanings in adulthood. Using the mermaid as a metaphor the collection traces the transformation of a figure once perceived as innocent, whimsical, and enchanting into one rooted in mythology where beauty coexists with seduction, power, and danger. This shift reflects the way understanding is shaped by experience uncovering the layers hidden beneath familiar narratives. Through sculptural silhouettes, handcrafted textiles, transparency, and fluid movement, each look represents a stage in this journey inviting the viewer to look beyond the surface and embrace the complexity that lies beneath.

Look 01 — Innocence

The Childhood Mermaid

The first look embodies the innocence of the mermaid as remembered through childhood stories and imagination. Constructed with transparent acrylic and adorned with vibrant hand embroidery inspired by underwater flora, the look captures a sense of playfulness, purity, and discovery. A sculptural wig extends the fantasy, enhancing the dreamlike quality of the silhouette and evoking the iconic image of the mermaid preserved in childhood memory. The light, youthful silhouette reflects a world where the ocean is magical, gentle and untouched by the darker narratives that follow.

Look 02 — Transformation

The Beginning of Mystery

The second look marks the beginning of transformation, where innocence gradually intertwines with mystery. A sculptural hoop silhouette inspired by the mermaid's hip shape introduces volume and movement while textured embellishments influenced by sea urchins and marine life create depth and intrigue. Soft yet structured, the garment represents the moment when fascination begins to replace familiarity, hinting at the seductive power hidden beneath the surface.

Look 03 — Allure

The Hypnotic Mermaid

The third look explores the hypnotic and alluring nature of the mythical mermaid. Wire structures inspired by the rhythm of ocean waves wrap around the body, while intricate beadwork and delicate threads create an illusion of movement and entrapment. The balance of transparency and structure reflects the tension between attraction and danger, expressing the irresistible pull of a beauty that conceals hidden power.

Look 04 — Power

The Mythical Form

The final look represents the mermaid in her most powerful and mythical form. A flowing ombré silk skirt evokes the depths of the ocean, while an organza corset layered with latex and textured to resemble sand blurs the boundary between the human body and the seascape. The same sand-like texture continues onto the skirt, creating a cohesive surface that reflects the shoreline where land and sea converge. Rich hand embroidery, intricate beadwork, and organic textures celebrate craftsmanship while echoing the shifting landscapes beneath the tide. Dark, dramatic, and ethereal, the look embodies the seductive yet dangerous essence of the mermaid bringing the narrative to its final evolution where beauty and peril become inseparable.
